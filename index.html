Tu Youyou

The woman who saved millions of lives

Tu Youyou (right) and her tutor Lou Zhicen in China Academy of Chinese Medical Sciences in 1950s.

Here's a time line of Tu Youyou's life:

  • 1930 - Born in NingBo, Zhejiang,China
  • 1951 - Attends Peking University Medical School
  • 1955 - Finishes university and continues her research on Chinese herbal medicine in the China Academy of Chinese Medical Sciences
  • 1969 - Becomes the head of the Project 523 research group at her institute and tries to find a treatment for malaria
  • 1972 - Obtaines the pure substance and named it qinghaosu, or artemisinin in English. This substance has now saved millions of lives, especially in the developing world.
  • 2015 - Receives the Nobel Prize in Medicine
